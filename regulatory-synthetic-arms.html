Next-Generation Regulatory Synthetic Arms

Methodological Blueprints for FedECA and IPTW Architectures

This page serves as a methodological whitepaper. All content strictly reflects a computational blueprint and an architectural proposal awaiting physical clinical integration.

Eradicating the Physical Placebo

The reliance on physical placebos in terminal neurodegenerative trials—specifically ALS and Lewy Body Dementia—introduces profound bioethical friction and severely bottlenecks patient enrollment. RZST proposes a mathematically rigorous, privacy-preserving substitute: the high-fidelity digital twin. We are architecting computational blueprints designed to replace the physical control group with a causally-validated synthetic baseline, transforming trial equity without sacrificing statistical power.

Federated External Control Arms (FedECA)

Traditional synthetic control arms fail because they attempt to pool highly sensitive, proprietary patient data into centralized databases—violating HIPAA and institutional data sovereignty.

Our blueprint resolves this through the proposed deployment of Federated External Control Arms (FedECA). Rather than moving the data, our architecture is engineered to send the algorithmic intelligence directly to the horizontally-partitioned clinical silos. The AI trains locally, learning the baseline disease progression of specific neurodegenerative cohorts, and aggregates only the de-identified mathematical weights. This establishes a legally compliant, highly scalable foundation for generating digital twins.

Inverse Probability of Treatment Weighting (IPTW)

To satisfy the highest standards of regulatory review, a synthetic control arm must perfectly mimic a randomized physical trial. Our in-silico models achieve this by applying Inverse Probability of Treatment Weighting (IPTW) to the federated data.

In our orchestration framework, this mathematical balancing enforces what we term "Living Coherence"—the system's ability to maintain essential statistical identity and purpose while continuously adapting to highly variable, chaotic real-world patient data. The foundational differential equation governing this adaptive stability within the predictive model is:

dS/dt = α(E) − βS

  • S — System coherence (the synthetic baseline)
  • E — Environmental pressure (patient covariate variance)
  • α — Adaptation rate
  • β — Stability coefficient

By applying these principles via IPTW, the architecture neutralizes selection bias and confounding variables, outputting a mathematically balanced digital twin cohort.

The FDA Plausible Mechanism Pathway

RZST does not operate outside of regulatory guardrails. This methodological blueprint is engineered in strict alignment with the FDA's ISTAND regulatory framework for New Approach Methodologies (NAMs) and the Plausible Mechanism pathway. By providing clinical hubs and BioDAOs with this in-silico architecture, we offer a computationally validated blueprint to modernize trial design, accelerate therapeutic validation, and advance equity for sensitive patient populations.
