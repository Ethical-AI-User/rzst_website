Closing the Computational Loop

Translating Digital Twins to Physical Microphysiological Systems

All content on this page strictly reflects computational models and architectural proposals.

The 99% Translational Gap

The traditional pharmaceutical pipeline is paralyzed by a profound "translational gap". Experimental compounds that demonstrate perfect binding affinities in computer simulations or exceptional efficacy in legacy animal models frequently fail catastrophically when administered to human patients. In complex neurodegenerative diseases, this failure rate approaches 99%. To solve this, RZST's architecture is engineered to bypass the animal model entirely.

Closing the Loop with Organ-on-a-Chip

While our current D-CLEF architecture represents a rigorously validated computational simulation, our future horizon is strictly physical. We have designed a high-fidelity pipeline that directly couples the predictive outputs of our Multi-scale Protein Language Models (PLMs) with living human biology.

  1. The Digital Output: The federated PLM predicts the exact molecular structure (SMILES string) optimized to bind selectively to toxic proteinopathies, such as fibrillar alpha-synuclein or aggregated TDP-43.
  2. Physical Synthesis & Integration: The proposed molecule is physically synthesized and introduced into patient-derived Organ-on-a-Chip Microphysiological Systems (MPS).
  3. The Empirical Readout: The microfluidic chip physically measures target engagement and screens for critical predictive toxicology (e.g., hepatotoxicity via DeepDILI, cardiotoxicity via hERG).
  4. Recursive Optimization: The physical biological data points generated by the chip are converted back into mathematical loss gradients, feeding directly back into the PLM to refine the next generation of molecules.

Mechanistic Proof for Regulatory Approval

This closed-loop system is not just an engineering marvel; it is a regulatory necessity. Under the FDA's Plausible Mechanism Framework, marketing approval requires incontrovertible proof of target engagement. By grounding our in-silico simulations with empirical in-vitro data from human microfluidics, we provide the exact mechanistic proof required by regulators. This architecture perfectly aligns with the NIH's July 2025 mandate to transition away from legacy animal testing, securing a fast-tracked, bioethically sound pathway to clinical translation.
